'No stone unturned' in Newspaper hacking probe
The Metropolitan Police says it will leave "no stone unturned" in its new investigation into phone hacking by newspaper journalists.Four years after a court case highlighted cases of phone hacking at the News of the World, Scotland Yard says it will investigate new claims that tabloid journalists have been intercepting the phone messages of prominent people.The case has been re-opened after officers received new information.The BBC's Home Affairs correspondent Tom Symonds reports.
